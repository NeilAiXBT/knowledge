## 3. Deep Understanding

#### Crucial Q&As

1. **What are Goroutines, and how do they differ from OS threads?**  
   Goroutines are lightweight managed threads enabling high concurrency, with minimal overhead compared to OS threads.
2. **Explain Go’s channel mechanism and its role in synchronization.**  
   Channels are typed conduits for safely sharing data between Goroutines, allowing for synchronization without explicit locking.
3. **What is escape analysis in Go, and why does it matter?**  
   Escape analysis decides whether variables reside on the stack or heap, significantly impacting memory efficiency and garbage collection.
4. **How do Go interfaces and embedding increase flexibility?**  
   Interfaces provide contracts for behavior, and embedding promotes composition for extensible type hierarchies.
5. **Describe how Go’s runtime scheduler manages blocking operations.**  
   The scheduler can reschedule goroutines so that blocked routines don’t prevent other tasks from being executed, ensuring efficient concurrency.
